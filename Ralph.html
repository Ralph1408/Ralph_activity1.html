Cyberbullying

cyberbullying

Cyberbullying includes sending, posting, or sharing negative, harmful, false, or mean content about someone else. It can include sharing personal or private information about someone else causing embarrassment or humiliation. Some cyberbullying crosses the line into unlawful or criminal behavior.

Learn how to avoid Cyberbullying

1. Be mindful of what you post online: Avoid posting personal information or provocative statements that could make you a target for cyberbullying.

2. Keep personal information private: Do not share personal information such as your address, phone number, or school name with strangers online.

3. Use privacy settings: On social media platforms, adjust your privacy settings to control who can see your posts and personal information.
